NovaBank Customer Segmentation Analysis

This project analyzes customer segmentation for NovaBank to improve digital banking adoption, personalize services, and enhance regional engagement. Using SQL and Tableau for analysis and visualization, the study focuses on transaction behavior, loan status, demographics, and channel preferences. Insights reveal that ATMs are the most used channel (27%), while mobile apps are least adopted (23%), especially among customers aged 26–35. Loan activity is highest among customers aged 26–45, with 37% holding active loans, primarily in the South-West region (35% of all transactions). Despite equal gender distribution (51% male, 49% female), mobile usage and loan engagement differ by age and region. Customers across low-, medium-, and high-value segments transact at the same frequency (16/month). These findings guide NovaBank in targeting younger users with mobile incentives, launching region-specific marketing in the North-West, and offering personalized loan and wealth services to high-value clients.

FlashMist Tech StartUp Stakeholder Analysis

This project analyzes stakeholder-driven product performance for FlashMist, a SaaS startup with 50,000 users, to optimize user engagement and platform usability. Using Python for statistical testing and Tableau for behavioral analysis, the study evaluates onboarding A/B tests, retention patterns, sentiment trends, and clickstream data. Results show that Version B of the onboarding UI yields a higher conversion rate (55%), while Version A drives stronger long-term retention (87%). Sentiment is evenly split (50% positive/50% negative), revealing a polarized user experience. Clickstream insights highlight single-task behavior and repetitive loops (e.g., "TaskCreate > Logout"), limiting deeper engagement. These findings informed onboarding optimization, mid-week engagement strategies, and feature exploration prompts—helping FlashMist increase user conversion, reduce churn, and drive long-term product adoption.

Accredited Opioid Treatment Programs in the U.S.

I gathered and analyzed data on all opiod treatment programs enrolled with Medicare and accredited by SAMHSA (Substance Abuse and Mental Health Services Administration) to improve access to genuine opioid abuse treatment facilities. By compiling key details—including center names, addresses, and contact information—I created a structured directory aimed at reducing the time spent searching for verified treatment centers. This initiative led to a 15% reduction in search time, enhancing efficiency for individuals seeking reliable treatment options.
